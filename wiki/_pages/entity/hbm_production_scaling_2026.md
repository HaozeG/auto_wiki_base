# HBM Production Scaling (2026)

High-bandwidth memory (HBM) production scaling in 2026 refers to the strategic expansion of manufacturing capacity by South Korean memory giants Samsung and SK Hynix to meet surging demand from artificial intelligence customers. Per a Reuters report cited by Data Center Dynamics, during Samsung's New Year address, co-CEO Jun Young-hyun stated that customers had expressed that "Samsung is back" in the context of HBM4, the next-generation HBM product. Both Samsung and SK Hynix are reportedly scaling up production of HBM and DRAM, with capacity effectively sold out into 2026 as data centers compete to secure memory for AI build-outs. SK Hynix has signaled plans to spend heavily to expand production and satisfy the runaway demand from the AI industry. This expansion underscores the critical role of memory in AI infrastructure, where HBM is essential for high-performance accelerators like NVIDIA's H100 and B200. The move also highlights the shifting dynamics in the semiconductor market, where memory supply is now a bottleneck for AI scaling.

## Key Claims
