# Esperanto Technologies

Esperanto Technologies is a Silicon Valley-based company founded in 2014 and headquartered in Mountain View, California, developing massively parallel RISC-V processors for artificial intelligence and high-performance computing workloads. The company was founded by Dave Ditzel, a veteran of Sun Microsystems' SPARC architecture and co-founder of Transmeta. Esperanto's flagship product, the ET-SoC-1, integrates over one thousand RISC-V cores (1,088 or 1,092 depending on the source) on a single die fabricated on TSMC's 7nm process, and is delivered as a compact, low-profile PCIe Gen 4 card. The company positions its technology as a high-efficiency alternative to NVIDIA GPUs for AI inference, claiming best-in-class total cost of ownership and energy efficiency for the hyperscaler datacenter market. More recently, Esperanto has also targeted generative AI workloads with its massively parallel architecture.

## Key Claims

- Founded in 2014, headquartered in Mountain View, California.
- Led by Dave Ditzel, co-designer of the SPARC processor and co-founder of Transmeta.
- Develops massively parallel RISC-V chips for AI inference and HPC.
- Flagship product ET-SoC-1 packs over 1000 RISC-V cores (1,088 or 1,092) on a single die.
- ET-SoC-1 fabricated on TSMC 7nm process.
- Available as a PCIe Gen 4 card in a compact, low-profile form factor.
- Claims to offer the highest RISC-V core density and parallelism in the industry.
- Targets hyperscaler datacenter inference workloads, including generative AI.
- Aims to beat NVIDIA at AI inference with significantly lower power consumption.
