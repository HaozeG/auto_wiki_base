# AMD Instinct

AMD Instinct is a brand of data center GPUs and accelerators designed and marketed by Advanced Micro Devices (AMD). Launched on June 20, 2017, the Instinct line replaced AMD's earlier FirePro S brand and is intended for high-performance computing (HPC), deep learning, artificial neural networks, and general-purpose GPU (GPGPU) applications. The product line directly competes with Nvidia's Tesla datacenter GPUs and Intel's Xeon Phi and Data Center GPU lines. Originally branded as "AMD Radeon Instinct," the Radeon prefix was dropped with the introduction of the MI100 in November 2020. The Instinct series spans multiple GPU architectures, including GCN 3, GCN 4, GCN 5, and AMD's CDNA (Compute DNA) generations used in the MI100 and later models. Key milestones include the MI300 series APU integrating Zen 4 CPU cores and CDNA 3 GPU cores, and the use of AMD Instinct-based systems like Frontier dominating the TOP500 and Green500 lists.

## Key Claims

- AMD Instinct was announced on December 12, 2016 and released on June 20, 2017.
- The MI6 features a Polaris 10 GPU with 5.7 TFLOPS FP16/FP32 and 358 GFLOPS FP64, <150 W TDP.
- The MI8 features a Fiji GPU with 8.2 TFLOPS FP16/FP32 and 512 GFLOPS FP64, <175 W TDP.
- The MI25 features a Vega GPU with 12.3 TFLOPS FP32 and 24.6 TFLOPS FP16, <300 W TDP.
- MI50 and MI60 are based on Vega20 (GCN 5) and support 1/2 rate FP64.
- MI100 uses CDNA 1 architecture with matrix processing units.
- MI250X achieves 383 TFLOPS FP16, 95.7 TFLOPS FP32, 47.9 TFLOPS FP64.
- MI300A is an APU integrating 24 Zen 4 CPU cores and 228 CDNA 3 GPU CUs, with 128 GB HBM3 memory.
- MI300X offers 304 CDNA 3 CUs and 192 GB HBM3 memory.
- MI325X introduced in October 2024 with HBM3E memory.
- MI350X planned for 2025 using CDNA 4 on 3 nm process.
- AMD Instinct-based supercomputers held top 4 spots on Green500 list in June 2022.
- Frontier supercomputer, using AMD Epyc CPUs and Instinct GPUs, became the fastest supercomputer in the world as of 2023.
