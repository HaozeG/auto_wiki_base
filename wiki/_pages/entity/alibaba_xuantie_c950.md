# Alibaba XuanTie C950

The Alibaba XuanTie C950 is a 5-nanometer (nm) RISC-V processor developed by Alibaba Group's DAMO Academy, introduced in March 2026 as the claimed most powerful RISC-V chip ever built. Running at 3.2 gigahertz (GHz), it delivers over three times the performance of its predecessor, the XuanTie C910, and natively supports Chinese AI models including Qwen3 and DeepSeek V3. The chip is part of China's strategic effort to build an independent semiconductor ecosystem using the open-source RISC-V architecture, thereby avoiding expensive licensing fees associated with proprietary designs (such as ARM) and bypassing United States export controls on high-performance chips. Alibaba does not sell the C950 externally but uses it internally for its cloud computing services. Production capacity remains a constraint, and China accounts for approximately 50% of global RISC-V shipments, reflecting a deliberate national strategy to reduce dependence on Western semiconductor ecosystems.
