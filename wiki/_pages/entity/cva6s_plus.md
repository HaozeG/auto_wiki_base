# CVA6S+

CVA6S+ is an enhanced dual-issue superscalar in-order RISC-V core derived from the open-source CVA6 architecture. Developed as part of a comparative study by ETH Zurich and the University of Bologna, it achieves a 34.4% improvement in instructions per cycle (IPC) over the single-issue CVA6 while incurring only a 6% area increase when implemented in GlobalFoundries 22FDX technology. CVA6S+ is designed to be industry-supported and compatible with standard electronic design automation (EDA) tools, addressing the adoption challenges of high-performance open-source RISC-V cores. The core leads in area efficiency measured as GOPS/mm² among the compared cores, demonstrating that modest superscalar enhancements can yield significant performance gains without disproportionate area or energy penalties.

## Key Claims

- CVA6S+ is a dual-issue superscalar in-order RISC-V core.
- Derived from the open-source CVA6 core.
- Achieves 34.4% IPC improvement over scalar CVA6.
- Area increase of 6% compared to CVA6.
- Leads in area efficiency (GOPS/mm²) among compared cores.
- Implemented in GF22FDX technology as part of Cheshire SoC platform.
- Designed to be industry-supported and EDA-tool compatible.
