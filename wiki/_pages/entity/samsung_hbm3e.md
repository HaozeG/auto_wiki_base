# Samsung HBM3E

HBM3E (High Bandwidth Memory 3E) is a high-bandwidth DRAM product developed by Samsung Semiconductor. It provides a bandwidth of up to 1,180 GB/s per stack and offers an industry-leading capacity of 36 GB per stack. As an enhanced iteration of the HBM3 standard, HBM3E is designed to meet the increasing memory demands of high-performance computing (HPC) and artificial intelligence (AI) accelerators. The product competes with similar offerings from SK Hynix and Micron, and is used in advanced GPU modules such as NVIDIA's H200 and B200 series. Samsung has positioned HBM3E as a key component for next-generation AI and supercomputing systems, emphasizing its high bandwidth and capacity relative to previous HBM generations. The product is currently in active production and represents a significant milestone in memory technology scaling.

## Key Claims

- Bandwidth up to 1,180 GB/s per stack, the highest among competing products.
- Capacity up to 36 GB per stack, industry-leading for HBM products.
- All-time high bandwidth and capacity for HBM DRAM.
- Active product status as of 2026.
- Designed for high-performance computing and AI accelerator workloads.
- Competing directly with SK Hynix HBM3E and Micron HBM3E offerings.

## Relationships
