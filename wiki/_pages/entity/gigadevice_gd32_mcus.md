# GigaDevice GD32 Microcontrollers

GigaDevice GD32 microcontrollers are a family of 32-bit general-purpose microcontrollers produced by GigaDevice, a Chinese semiconductor company. The GD32 family encompasses multiple series based on both Arm Cortex-M and RISC-V processor cores, offering a range of performance levels from high-performance (e.g., GD32H7xx series) to mainstream (e.g., GD32F30x series) and entry-level (e.g., GD32C2x1 series) options. Additionally, the portfolio includes low-power consumption MCUs (GD32L23x series), wireless MCUs and modules (GD32VW553, GD32W515 series), and automotive-grade MCUs (GD32A5xx, GD32A7xx series). These microcontrollers are designed for applications such as industrial control, motor control, energy systems, robotics, the Internet of Things (IoT), health monitoring, home appliances, and automotive electronics. The GD32 family provides extensive peripheral integration, scalable architecture, and is supported by GigaDevice's development tools and ecosystem.

## Key Claims

- GigaDevice GD32 MCUs are based on Arm Cortex-M and RISC-V architectures.
- The portfolio includes high-performance series like GD32H7xx and GD32G5xx.
- Mainstream series include GD32F30x, GD32F10x, GD32C1x3, GD32E1x3, GD32E502, and GD32VF103.
- Entry-level series include GD32C2x1, GD32E23x, GD32F3x0, GD32F1x0.
- Low-power consumption series: GD32L23x.
- Wireless MCUs: GD32VW553 and GD32W515 series.
- Automotive MCUs: GD32A5xx and GD32A7xx series.
- Specific MCUs: GD32M531, GD32E512/511/501, GD32E252/251/232, GD32EPRT, GD32FFPR.
- GD32 MCUs target applications in industrial control, motor control, energy, robotics, IoT, health monitoring, home appliances, automotive.
- GigaDevice provides development tools and ecosystem support.
