# XuanTie C930

The XuanTie C930 is Alibaba's first server-grade RISC-V processor, designed for artificial intelligence and high-performance computing (AI-HPC) workloads. Announced by Alibaba's Damo Academy research institute at a conference in Beijing in March 2025, it is the latest addition to the XuanTie family of RISC-V cores. While detailed specifications such as core counts, clock speeds, and cache sizes have not been officially disclosed, Alibaba has described the C930 as a server-grade chip intended to compete with established architectures like Intel Xeon and AMD EPYC. The processor is expected to begin shipping in March 2025. Some Chinese publications have cited a rumored performance of 15 points per GHz in the legacy SPECint2006 benchmark, but these numbers have not been confirmed and appear to originate from earlier rumours around the chip. The C930 marks Alibaba's entry into the RISC-V server CPU market, leveraging the open-source ISA to circumvent U.S. export restrictions on more established instruction set architectures.

## Key Claims

- First server-grade RISC-V processor from Alibaba.
- Designed for AI-HPC workloads.
- Announced by Damo Academy in March 2025; expected to ship in March 2025.
- No official core counts, clock speeds, or cache sizes disclosed.
- Rumoured performance of 15 points per GHz in SPECint2006 (unconfirmed, from Chinese publications).
- Part of the XuanTie family, with future announced chips including the C908X (AI acceleration), R908A (automotive), and XL200 (high-speed connectivity).
- Alibaba's Damo Academy claims its CPU team has supported more than 30% of RISC-V high-performance processors.
