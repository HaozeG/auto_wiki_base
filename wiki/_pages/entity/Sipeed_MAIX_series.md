# Sipeed MAIX series

The Sipeed MAIX series is a family of low-cost, open-source development boards and modules designed for edge artificial intelligence (AI) and Internet of Things (IoT) applications, centered around the Kendryte K210 dual-core 64-bit RISC-V processor with an integrated Neural Processing Unit (NPU). Developed by Sipeed Technology, a Shenzhen-based Chinese hardware company founded in 2015, the series was launched via crowdfunding in late 2018 and includes models such as Maix Bit, Maix Dock, and Maixduino, emphasizing ease of use with MicroPython and MaixPy frameworks. These boards leverage the K210's machine vision and hearing capabilities, enabling low-power, zero-threshold AI development without proprietary ecosystems, and support applications in computer vision, voice interaction, AI education, and AIoT prototyping.

## Key Claims

- The MAIX series uses the Kendryte K210 SoC with dual-core 64-bit RISC-V CPU at 400 MHz and integrated NPU.
- Series launched via crowdfunding in late 2018 by Sipeed Technology.
- Models include Maix Bit, Maix Dock, Maixduino, Maix Go, Maix Cube, Maix Amigo, Maix Nano.
- Supports machine vision (object detection, face recognition, image classification) and machine hearing (speech recognition, sound localization).
- Software support includes MicroPython and MaixPy frameworks.
- General specs: dual-core RISC-V at 400 MHz, 8 MB on-chip SRAM, up to 128 MB external flash.
- Later iterations include the MAIX II and III series.
