# Intel Xeon W9-3495X

The Intel Xeon W9-3495X is a 56-core high-end desktop (HEDT) processor based on the Golden Cove microarchitecture, part of the Intel Sapphire Rapids platform. It supports eight channels of DDR5 memory and is designed for workstation and enthusiast applications. The processor has been demonstrated reaching an all-core frequency of 5.5 GHz under extreme liquid nitrogen cooling, consuming up to 1881 watts of power and achieving a Cinebench R23 multi-core score of 132,220 points, demonstrating exceptional performance capability. It was launched in early 2023 as the flagship of the Xeon W-3400 series, targeting professional content creation, scientific computing, and highly multi-threaded workloads.

## Key Claims

- Features 56 physical cores (112 threads) based on Intel's Golden Cove architecture.
- Official default TDP is 350W, but overclocking can push power consumption to 1881W under extreme cooling.
- Achieved an all-core overclock of 5.5 GHz using liquid nitrogen cooling, on an ASUS W790E-SAGE SE motherboard with G.SKILL Zeta R5 DDR5 memory.
- Scored 132,220 points in Cinebench R23 multi-core benchmark, close to the world record of 132,484.
- Supports 8-channel DDR5 memory with up to 4800 MT/s officially, though overclocked memory may exceed.
- Part of the Intel Sapphire Rapids platform, which introduces PCIe 5.0 and improved memory bandwidth.
