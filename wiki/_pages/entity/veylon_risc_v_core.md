# Veyron (RISC-V Core)

The Veyron is a family of high-performance RISC-V processor cores developed by Tenstorrent, an AI chip startup led by industry veteran Jim Keller. The architecture targets both general-purpose computing and artificial intelligence acceleration, offering a RISC-V alternative to ARM-based designs such as Apple's M-series processors. According to discussions on MacRumors forums, the Veyron V1 is claimed to achieve up to 6 points in the SPEC CPU 2017 integer benchmark (SPECint2017), a standard measure of processor performance. Its successor, the Veyron V2, is reported to deliver a 40% performance improvement, pushing the estimated score to approximately 8.5 points — a level that would place it on par with Apple's M2 chip. These claims, if verified, would mark a significant milestone for RISC-V in the high-performance computing space and suggest increasing viability for integration into Apple's silicon roadmap, though no official announcements have been made.

## Key Claims

- The Veyron V1 achieves up to 6 points in SPECint2017.
- The Veyron V2, with a 40% uplift, reaches approximately 8.5 points in SPECint2017.
- Veyron V2 performance is claimed to be comparable to Apple's M2 processor.
- The Veyron core is a RISC-V design that could potentially be adopted by Apple for future custom silicon.
