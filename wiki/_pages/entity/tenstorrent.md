# Tenstorrent

Tenstorrent is a developer of artificial intelligence chips that combines custom Tensix compute cores, AI accelerators, RISC-V processor intellectual property, systems, and compiler technologies into integrated AI solutions. Founded by industry veteran Jim Keller, the company has gained attention for its open-source approach to RISC-V and its focus on high-performance AI inference and training. As of mid-2026, Tenstorrent is reportedly in acquisition negotiations with Qualcomm, with a potential valuation between $8 billion and $10 billion. This would represent a major consolidation in the AI semiconductor space, merging Qualcomm's mobile chip expertise with Tenstorrent's RISC-V-based AI accelerator architecture. The company's Tensix architecture is central to its value proposition, offering a flexible, programmable platform for machine learning workloads.

## Key Claims

- Qualcomm is reportedly in advanced talks to acquire Tenstorrent for $8–10 billion, with the deal potentially including cash and stock.
- Tenstorrent's technology stack includes Tensix compute cores, AI accelerators, RISC-V processor IP, and associated systems and compiler tools.
- The acquisition would give Qualcomm a strong position in RISC-V-based AI acceleration, a key area for future AI hardware.
- Reports from Reuters and The Information, dated June 15, 2026, indicate negotiations are ongoing.
- Tenstorrent's valuation reflects its promise as an AI chip startup despite being pre-revenue or early stage (implied by acquisition interest).
