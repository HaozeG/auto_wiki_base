# XuanTie C930

Alibaba's DAMO Academy announced the XuanTie C930 at a conference in Beijing on February 28, 2025, marking the company's entry into server-grade RISC-V processors. Described as its 'first server-grade processor', the C930 is built for AI-HPC workloads and is part of the XuanTie RISC-V processor series. Alibaba first teased the C930 in 2024 with a planned 2024 release, but it launched in early 2025. The chip is set to ship to clients starting in March 2025. While detailed specifications such as core counts, clock speeds, and cache sizes have not been disclosed, unconfirmed rumors from Chinese publications suggest a SPECint2006 performance of 15 points per GHz.

## Key Claims

- The XuanTie C930 is Alibaba's first server-grade RISC-V processor, announced by DAMO Academy on February 28, 2025.
- It is designed for AI-HPC workloads and ships to clients starting in March 2025.
- Unconfirmed performance rumors claim 15 points per GHz in the SPECint2006 benchmark.
- Alongside the C930, Alibaba announced plans for three additional RISC-V chips: the C908X (AI acceleration), R908A (automotive), and XL200 (high-speed connectivity).
- Alibaba's DAMO Academy claims its CPU team has supported the implementation of over 30% of RISC-V high-performance processors.
