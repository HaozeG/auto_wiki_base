# Falcon Shores

Falcon Shores was a next-generation data-center accelerator platform developed by Intel as the successor to the canceled Rialto Bridge GPU and the follow-up to the Ponte Vecchio architecture. Originally announced as a hybrid CPU-GPU XPU for 2024, the project was repeatedly delayed and retargeted. By 2023, Intel had revised Falcon Shores to a pure GPU architecture, aiming for a 2025 launch. In January 2025, Intel announced that Falcon Shores would not be released as a commercial product but would instead serve as an internal test vehicle for architecture validation. The cancelled product was subsequently replaced by a new architecture, codenamed Jaguar Shores, which Intel is targeting for a later unspecified date. The decision left Intel without a competitive data-center AI accelerator for at least one to two years, according to industry analysts.

## Key Claims

- Intel canceled the Rialto Bridge GPU, which was planned as a 160 Xe-core follow-up to Ponte Vecchio, shifting focus to Falcon Shores.
- Falcon Shores was originally conceived as an XPU (hybrid CPU-GPU) for 2024, later redesigned as a standalone GPU for 2025.
- In January 2025, Intel confirmed Falcon Shores would not launch commercially; it will be used only for internal testing and validation.
- Intel's next data-center GPU architecture is codenamed Jaguar Shores, with no announced launch date.
- The cancellation leaves a gap in Intel's AI accelerator portfolio, with no production GPU product expected until Jaguar Shores or later.
