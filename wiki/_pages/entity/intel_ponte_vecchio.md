# Intel Ponte Vecchio

Intel Ponte Vecchio, also known as the Intel Max GPU, is a high-performance computing (HPC) accelerator designed by Intel for exascale-class workloads. Released in 2022, the GPU was built using Intel's multi-tile packaging technology, combining 47 tiles including compute, memory, and I/O chiplets, and delivered up to 45 TFLOPS of double-precision performance. In May 2024, Intel announced that Ponte Vecchio would no longer be marketed for new cluster deployments and would enter a support-only phase, with future development efforts converging onto the Falcon Shores architecture. The device was noted for its novel architecture and high complexity, but it was ultimately considered ahead of its time and struggled with market adoption compared to competitors like NVIDIA. The shift to support mode reflects Intel's strategic pivot toward a unified GPU architecture for AI and HPC, leaving Ponte Vecchio as a short-lived but technically ambitious product.

## Key Claims

- Ponte Vecchio entered a support-only phase in May 2024, ceasing new cluster deployments.
