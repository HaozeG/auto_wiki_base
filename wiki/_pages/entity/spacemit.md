# SpacemiT

SpacemiT (进迭时空) is a computing-chip company based in Hangzhou, China, founded in 2021. The company focuses on designing processors based on the RISC-V instruction set architecture, targeting artificial intelligence workloads (AI CPUs). SpacemiT has developed several system-on-chip (SoC) products including the M1, Key Stone K1, VitalStone V100, and the K3. In 2024, the company unveiled the Muse Book laptop powered by its Key Stone K1 octa-core RISC-V chip and running the Bianbu OS. In January 2025, SpacemiT announced the VitalStone V100 server processor with up to 64 RISC-V cores fabricated on a 12nm-class process, supporting virtualization and an IOMMU compliant with the RISC-V architecture. In July 2025, the company announced the K3 SoC based on the RVA23-profile-compliant X100 core, configurable in clusters of up to 64 cores. SpacemiT also sells consumer electronics under the Muse trademark, including clocks, audio devices, and DVD players.
