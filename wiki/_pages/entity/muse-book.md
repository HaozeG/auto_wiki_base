# Muse Book

The Muse Book is a RISC-V laptop developed by SpacemiT, powered by the K1 octa-core RISC-V SoC. It is designed for hardware engineers and DIY enthusiasts. The laptop features a 14.1-inch IPS display with 1920x1080 resolution, up to 16GB of LPDDR4X memory, 32GB eMMC flash storage (expandable via M.2 NVMe), and a 36.48Wh battery supporting USB PD 3.1 fast charging with an estimated battery life of over 6 hours. It offers multiple I/O ports including USB 3.2 Type-C with PD and DP, USB 3.0 Type-A, a microSD card slot, and an 8-pin expansion header with I2C, UART, PWM, and GPIO. The laptop runs Bianbu OS, a Debian-based distribution optimized for the K1 SoC. It has a fanless all-metal design weighing approximately 1.36 kg. An expected price around $300 positions it as an affordable entry into RISC-V development.

## Key Claims

- First laptop to use the SpacemiT K1 octa-core RISC-V SoC.
- 14.1-inch IPS display at 1920×1080 resolution with 60 Hz refresh rate.
- Up to 16GB LPDDR4X memory.
- 36.48Wh battery with USB PD 3.1 fast charging and >6 hours battery life.
- 8-pin expansion header for hardware development (I2C, UART, PWM, GPIO).
- Fanless all-metal design, thickness ~17.8mm, weight ~1.36 kg.
- Runs Bianbu OS (Debian-based) with support for mainline Linux.
- Expected retail price around $300.
