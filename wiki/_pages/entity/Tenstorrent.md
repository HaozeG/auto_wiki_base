# Tenstorrent

Tenstorrent is an AI chip startup founded by microprocessor architect Jim Keller, developing high-performance RISC-V CPUs and AI accelerators. In September 2025, the company announced it had productized its RISC-V CPU and AI cores as licensable intellectual property (IP), a strategic shift from primarily selling hardware compute to offering semiconductor IP. Tenstorrent already counts LG and Hyundai among its IP licensees, with most of the company’s bookings to date coming from IP deals. The next-generation core architecture, named Neo, adopts a cluster architecture that shares resources, contrasting with the current generation Tensix-Blackhole core. The company positions its AI IP as scalable from one to over 1,000 cores, and has attracted interest from hyperscaler data center operators, with Qualcomm also reportedly considering acquisition.
