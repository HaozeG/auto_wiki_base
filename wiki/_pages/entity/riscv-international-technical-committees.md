# RISC-V International Technical Committees

The RISC-V International Technical Committees and Working Groups form the governance structure for developing and maintaining the RISC-V instruction set architecture (ISA) and its associated specifications. The Technical Steering Committee (TSC) serves as the primary technical governance body, consisting of task group chairs and Premier member representatives, with a hard limit of one representative per company. Below the TSC, the ISA Committees (ICs) oversee ISA extension task groups, while Horizontal Committees (HCs) ensure coverage of non-extension areas and that all extension groups address relevant cross-cutting concerns. The Certification Steering Committee (CSC) manages certification programs and is open to all members. Task Groups produce work products such as specifications, and Special Interest Groups (SIGs) facilitate discussion without producing deliverables. All technical work is open and publicly archived, with active participation limited to RISC-V International members.

## Key Claims

- The Technical Steering Committee (TSC) is the technical governance body, with voting members including Premier members and HC/IC chairs, limited to one representative per company.
- The Certification Steering Committee (CSC) governs certification programs and is open to all members, unlike the TSC.
- ISA Committees (ICs) approve and oversee ISA Extension Task Groups and fill chair/vice-chair vacancies.
- Horizontal Committees (HCs) ensure coverage of non-extension TGs and that all Extension TGs address their areas before ratification.
- Task Groups (TGs) must have a charter defining deliverables (specifications, extensions, standards, etc.); those under ICs may produce ISA extension work products, while those under HCs generally do not.
- Special Interest Groups (SIGs) are discussion forums without work products.
- The Technical Chairs Meeting and Technical Operations Meeting are distinct governance meetings with different invitees and purposes.
- All technical work is open with publicly visible archives; active working group participation is limited to members.
- Contributions are assigned to RISC-V International as community property; no individual retains ownership.
- Task groups that achieve final deliverables are "graduated" and archived; archived groups from before 2020 are stored in separate "groupname-archive" groups.
- The groups.io platform is used for mailing lists, calendars, file storage, and wiki pages; members can join subgroups freely.
- Non-members can see calendar entries but not meeting information.
